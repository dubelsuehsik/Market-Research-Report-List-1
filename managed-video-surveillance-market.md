<p><p>The managed video surveillance market comprises two primary types: Analog Video Surveillance Systems and IP Video Surveillance Systems. Analog systems use traditional coaxial cables to transmit video signals and are generally cost-effective for basic surveillance needs. In contrast, IP video surveillance systems utilize internet protocols for real-time video transmission, offering higher resolution, scalability, and advanced features like remote access and analytics. As businesses increasingly prioritize security, the IP systems are gaining popularity due to their superior capabilities and flexibility.</p></p>
