<p><p>The chili oil market is primarily divided into hot pressed and cold pressed varieties. Hot pressed chili oil is made by infusing hot peppers in heated oil, resulting in a robust, spicy flavor, often used in cooking and as a condiment. Cold pressed chili oil, on the other hand, is produced by gently extracting oil from chili peppers without heat, preserving more natural flavors and health benefits. Both types cater to diverse culinary preferences and consumer demands in the market.</p></p>
