<p><ul><li>Acidic</li><li>Alkaline</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Concrete Paint Remover market is primarily divided into two types: acidic and alkaline removers. Acidic removers, typically containing strong acids, effectively break down and dissolve paint layers on concrete surfaces, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications. In contrast, alkaline removers use basic substances to soften and lift paint, making them gentler on surfaces and preferred for delicate materials or environments. Both types serve distinct needs in concrete maintenance and restoration, catering to varying levels of paint adhesion and surface conditions.</p></p>
