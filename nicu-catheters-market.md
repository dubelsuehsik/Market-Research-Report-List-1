<p><p>The NICU catheters market consists of various types essential for neonatal care. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICCs) are long flexible tubes used for extended intravenous access, while Central Venous Catheters (CVCs) provide central access for medication administration and nutrition. Umbilical Venous Catheters (UVCs) are specifically designed for newborns, inserting into the umbilical vein for immediate vascular access. Other catheters include specialty devices catering to specific clinical needs, enhancing the management of critically ill neonates in neonatal intensive care units.</p></p>
