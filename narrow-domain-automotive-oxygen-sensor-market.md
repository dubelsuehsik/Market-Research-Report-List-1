<p><p>The narrow domain automotive oxygen sensor market primarily features two types: Titanium Oxide and Zirconia sensors. Titanium Oxide sensors operate by detecting the presence of oxygen through changes in electrical resistance, offering rapid response times mainly for lean-burn engines. In contrast, Zirconia sensors utilize a ceramic zirconium dioxide element that measures oxygen levels based on voltage differences, making them effective for both rich and lean mixtures. Each type plays a crucial role in optimizing engine performance and reducing emissions.</p></p>
